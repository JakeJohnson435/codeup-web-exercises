Arches Natl. Park

  • There are over 2,000 recorded arches in the Park.
  • When a new arch in the park is discovered or formed the person to find it gets to name it.
  • The whole of the Park used to be under an inland sea which formed and re-formed 29 times.
  • One of the most iconic features in the Park is actually the Balanced Rock, and not an arch.

Yosemite Natl. Park

  • The campaign to get Yosemite to be a Natl Park was headed by a Scottish Immigrant.
  • The iconic Park Ranger hat known as the Montana Peak style was created by the Buffalo Soldiers who patrolled the park.
  • One of the biggest attractions of the Park used to be a "Waterfall of fire".
  • Yosemite is home to some of the most well known Big Wall climbs in the world. Including the Dawn Wall, once considered impossible to climb.

Zion Natl. Park

  • Despite looking peaceful, Angel's Landing is listed as one of the most dangerous trails in the world.
  • Alongside Arches Natl. Park, Zion has one of the largest freestanding arches in the world.
  • Zion used to be in the middle of a desert and had 2,000 foot thick sand dunes covering it.
  • There is an underground spring in the park which takes 1,000 years for the water to reach the surface through the sandstone.
